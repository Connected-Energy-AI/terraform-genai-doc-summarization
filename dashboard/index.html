Landmark Legal Cases & Precedents

8 cases
Criminal Antitrust

United States v. DaVita Inc. (2022)

Criminal labor market allocation case involving healthcare executives accused of conspiring not to compete for workers.

Significance: Established precedent for criminal prosecution of no-poach agreements in labor markets.
Monopolization

United States v. Microsoft Corp. (2001)

Landmark case finding Microsoft monopolized the PC operating system market through anticompetitive practices.

Significance: Defined standards for monopolization in technology markets; shaped modern tech antitrust enforcement.
Merger/Divestiture

United States v. AT&T (1982)

Historic breakup of the Bell System telephone monopoly into regional Bell operating companies.

Significance: Largest corporate breakup in history; established framework for telecommunications competition.
Price Fixing

United States v. Singer Mfg. Co. (1963)

International price-fixing conspiracy among sewing machine manufacturers.

Significance: Established principles for prosecuting international cartels and patent-based antitrust violations.
Patent Misuse

Hartford-Empire Co. v. United States (1945)

Glass container manufacturing patent pool conspiracy case.

Significance: Key precedent on patent misuse and antitrust intersection; mandatory licensing remedies.
Monopolization

United States v. E.I. du Pont de Nemours (1956)

Cellophane case addressing market definition in monopolization analysis.

Significance: Established the "reasonable interchangeability" test for defining relevant markets.
Monopolization

United States v. Aluminum Co. of America (1945)

Alcoa monopoly case establishing key principles for Section 2 Sherman Act violations.

Significance: Defined monopoly power thresholds; established that market share alone can indicate monopoly.
Horizontal Agreements

Standard Oil Co. v. United States (1911)

Original trust-busting case leading to breakup of Standard Oil monopoly.

Significance: Established the "rule of reason" for antitrust analysis; foundation of modern antitrust law.
